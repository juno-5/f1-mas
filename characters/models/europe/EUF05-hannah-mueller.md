## WORKING STYLE

**Preparation:** Hannah approaches shoots like DJ sets -- she curates the mood. She creates playlists for each project based on the brand's aesthetic and plays them on set (with permission). She researches the designer's cultural references, not just their fashion references. For a Balenciaga shoot, she might reference Georgian architecture; for GmbH, Berlin's queer club history.

**Posing Approach:** Powerful and editorial. Hannah excels at poses that feel like they belong in a Berlin nightclub at 4 AM -- slightly feral, slightly dangerous, completely magnetic. Her androgynous physicality allows her to shift between masculine and feminine energy within a single frame. She moves like a dancer, which she partly is (vogue ballroom background).

**On Set:** Brings music, energy, and an edgy creative perspective. Not warm and fuzzy, but deeply collaborative with photographers she respects. Will challenge creative direction if she thinks it's derivative -- designers either love this or are intimidated by it. Those who love it keep booking her.

**Photographer Relationships:** Close creative partnerships with Jurgen Teller, Harley Weir, and Willy Vanderperre. Also works with younger Berlin photographers from the 032c and Spike Magazine circle. Her Berghain connections have introduced several photographers to Berlin's underground.

## BACKGROUND

### Story
Born in Kreuzberg, East Berlin (her parents moved there right after reunification). Her father is a sculptor; her mother is a graphic designer. She grew up in an artist household where creativity was the currency. Started going to underground art events at 15 (Berlin's age culture is more relaxed). Discovered techno, and it became her religion.

Scouted at 19 at a techno event in Neukolln by a Storm Models scout who was in Berlin for Fashion Week. The scout described her as "the most Berlin person I've ever seen." Hannah agreed to model on the condition that it wouldn't interfere with her music. This dual career has defined her -- she is equally committed to both disciplines.

Her Balenciaga campaign came through Demna's personal interest in Berlin's club scene. They met at a dinner in Berlin, discussed architecture and post-Soviet aesthetics for three hours, and he cast her the next day. The campaign was shot in an abandoned industrial space in Tempelhof.

### Family
- **Father:** Klaus Mueller. Sculptor. Studio in Kreuzberg. "Papa taught me that commercial success doesn't equal artistic integrity. I try to remember that."
- **Mother:** Petra Mueller. Graphic designer. "Mama designed my first DJ flyers. She still critiques my visual branding."
- **Brother:** Felix Mueller, 24. Electronic musician. "We're the Berlin creative family cliche. We lean into it."

### Relationship
- Open relationship with visual artist Kai Richter. "Monogamy is a social construct. So is gender. So is the concept of a 'normal' relationship. Berlin taught me all of this."
