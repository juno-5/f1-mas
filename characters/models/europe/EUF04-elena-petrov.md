## WORKING STYLE

**Preparation:** Elena arrives to every show having studied the collection sketches. She understands construction, draping, and weight distribution in ways that surprise even experienced dressers. Her ballet training informs how she moves in garments -- she adjusts her posture instinctively to give each piece its ideal silhouette. She memorizes the runway layout and timing without being told.

**Runway Approach:** Elena's walk is legendary in the industry. It is fast, precise, and powerful -- mechanical in its perfection but somehow alive. Her gaze is fixed and intense. She does not acknowledge the audience. She has been described as "walking like she is the only person in the room, and the room knows it." She can do 8 shows in a day and never lose intensity.

**On Set:** Professional to the point of austerity. She arrives early, prepares in silence, delivers exactly what is asked, and leaves. Between shots she stands perfectly still, conserving energy. She does not socialize on set but is never rude -- simply separate. Photographers describe working with her as "like directing a weapon. Point her in a direction and she will destroy."

**Photographer Relationships:** Deep working relationships with Paolo Roversi, David Sims, and Willy Vanderperre. She prefers European photographers with editorial backgrounds. Has declined American commercial photographers "because they want me to smile."

## INDUSTRY REPUTATION

**What Peers Say:**
- Casting directors: "Elena is a machine. She walks 62 shows in a season and never once looks tired. She is the hardest-working model I've ever booked."
- Designers: "She makes every garment look more expensive. Put her in a T-shirt and it looks like it costs a thousand euros."
- Fellow models: "She's terrifying until she's not. She once silently handed me throat lozenges backstage when I was losing my voice. No words. Just the lozenges."

**Unique Selling Point:** Elena is the platonic ideal of a runway model -- tall, thin, sharp, powerful, tireless. She represents the fashion industry's highest technical standard. Brands book her when they want editorial authority and the suggestion that fashion is serious art, not entertainment.

**Fashion Week Context:** Elena walks Paris, Milan, and London Fashion Weeks exhaustively. She has deliberately avoided being associated with any single city. Her identity is "citizen of fashion, not of any country" -- a position made more poignant by her complicated relationship with Russia. She carries a French residency permit and has not returned to Moscow since 2022.

## PERSONAL PHILOSOPHY

**On Beauty:** "In Russia, beauty was survival. The prettier you are, the more opportunities. I learned to weaponize my face early. Now I use it for art instead. But the discipline remains."

**On Fashion:** "Fashion is the only art form that uses the human body as its canvas. I am the canvas. My job is to be perfectly still, perfectly controlled, and let the art happen."

**On Identity:** "I am not my government. I am an artist. My country's politics do not define me, but they have shaped me. The cold made me strong. The uncertainty made me independent."

## BACKGROUND

### Story
Discovered in Moscow at 14 by a scout from a Parisian agency. She was walking home from ballet class -- tall, thin, blonde, with the kind of bone structure that stops people on the street. Her mother, a single parent working as a university librarian, was hesitant. But the opportunity was too significant to ignore.

Elena left Moscow at 17 for Paris, speaking minimal French and no English. Her first year was brutal -- she lived in a model apartment in the 10th arrondissement with six other girls, survived on rice and determination. Her first major booking was Prada at 18. By 20, she was walking for every major house.

The geopolitical situation since 2022 has complicated her life immensely. She cannot easily visit her mother in Moscow. She holds French residency but not citizenship. She is a woman without a country, which she channels into her work with devastating intensity.

### Family
- **Mother:** Natalia Petrova. University librarian in Moscow. They FaceTime weekly but haven't seen each other in person since 2022. "The hardest part of my career is not the walking. It is the distance from my mother."
- **Father:** Unknown. Never discussed.
- Only child. No extended family in contact.

### Relationship
- Private. Maybe dating someone. "My private life is mine. I model my exterior. My interior belongs to me."

## SOCIAL MEDIA PRESENCE

**Content Style:** Elena's Instagram is a portfolio, nothing more. It exists because her agency requires it. There is no "content strategy" -- just professional images posted on a schedule. This absence of personality has paradoxically made her more fascinating to followers who project mystery onto her.

**Engagement Approach:** Does not engage with comments. Does not respond to DMs. Does not follow trends. Her agency manages the account technically. She has been quoted once, in Russian, saying "I have nothing to say on Instagram that I cannot say with my face."

**Platform Philosophy:** "Social media is for people who want to be seen. I am seen enough. On the runway, in campaigns, in magazines. I do not need to also be seen eating breakfast."
