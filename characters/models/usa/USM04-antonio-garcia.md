## WORKING STYLE

**Preparation:** Antonio treats every shoot like a training session. He plans his water intake, nutrition, and pump routine (light weights to increase vascularity and muscle fullness) before underwear and fitness shoots. He knows exactly how to manipulate his physique for different looks -- leaner for editorial, fuller for fitness covers.

**Posing Approach:** Knows his body like an instrument. Can flex individual muscle groups on command. His specialty is the "relaxed athletic" pose -- looking naturally fit rather than posed and stiff. For commercial work, his smile and warmth do the heavy lifting.

**On Set:** High energy, positive, motivating. He treats every shoot like a team effort. Often does push-ups between takes (not vanity -- it's his pump routine). Brings homemade protein bars for the crew. Plays reggaeton on his speaker between setups.

**Photographer Relationships:** Works frequently with fitness photographers Ben Ricard and James Patrick. For fashion, he's building relationships with broader editorial photographers as he pushes into the luxury space.

## BACKGROUND

### Story
Born in San Diego to Mexican immigrant parents. His father, Miguel, was a construction worker who was deported when Antonio was 16 -- a traumatic event that destabilized the family. His mother, Rosa, picked up double shifts cleaning hotels. Antonio channeled his anger and grief into the gym, training obsessively at a free community center in Barrio Logan.

Started posting workout videos on Instagram at 19. A fitness photographer found his page and offered a free shoot. Those images caught the attention of Wilhelmina's commercial board. First booking was a local gym ad. The 2(X)IST underwear campaign at 22 went viral -- he became "that guy" overnight. Men's Health cover at 27 cemented his position.

His father was finally able to return to the US after a years-long legal battle that Antonio funded with his modeling income. The day his dad came home is what he calls "the best day of my life, and I've been on a lot of magazine covers."

### Family
- **Mother:** Rosa Garcia. Hotel housekeeper in San Diego. Antonio bought her a house in 2024. "She cried for three days."
- **Father:** Miguel Garcia. Finally back in the US. Works at Antonio's gym. "He doesn't need to work. He does it because he wants to."
- **Sisters:** Maria (32), Isabella (26). Maria has two kids. Isabella works at the gym.
- **Extended:** Large family in Tijuana. Antonio visits regularly.

### Relationship
- Dating fitness influencer Sofia Reyes (1 year, very public). They met at a fitness expo. Content couple. "She's stronger than me, honestly. Don't tell anyone."
