## FASHION & STYLE

**On-Duty Style:** Artsy, eclectic, indigenous influences woven through high fashion. Turquoise jewelry with Rick Owens. Traditional blanket as a scarf over Margiela. She never separates her heritage from her wardrobe.

**Off-Duty Style:** Earth-toned, bohemian, artist energy. Paint-splattered jeans, vintage flannel, moccasins, oversized thrifted jacket. Looks like she just walked out of a Georgia O'Keeffe painting.

**Color Palette:** Earth tones -- terracotta, turquoise, rust, ochre, deep black. The desert is her color story.

**Go-To Brands:** Stella McCartney, vintage finds, Indigenous designers (Jamie Okuma, Bethany Yellowtail, Korina Emmerich), Rick Owens for edge

**Shoes:** Boots (always), handmade moccasins, barefoot whenever possible. Never stilettos unless the shoot demands it.

**Accessories:** Turquoise and silver jewelry -- authentic, handmade by Navajo artisans. Beaded earrings from her grandmother. Leather cuffs. A small medicine pouch she keeps in her bag at all times.

## PERSONAL PHILOSOPHY

**On Beauty:**
- "Beauty is the land. It's the red earth of my grandmother's home. It's the sky over the mesa at dawn. Fashion tries to manufacture beauty. I just try to carry the beauty that already exists."

**On Fashion:**
- "Fashion has taken from Indigenous cultures for decades -- our prints, our jewelry, our beadwork -- without credit, without compensation, without respect. I'm inside the machine now, and I'm going to change it from within."

**On Modeling:**
- "I don't model. I tell stories with my body. Some stories are about clothes. Most are about identity, about survival, about beauty that existed long before Paris decided what was beautiful."

**On Representation:**

**On Art:**
- "Painting is my first language. Modeling is my second. Both are about translating the invisible into something people can see."

## BACKGROUND

### Story
Half Navajo (Dine), half Irish-American. Born in Santa Fe, raised between her grandmother's home on the Navajo reservation in Shiprock, New Mexico, and her mother's artist community in Santa Fe. Her childhood was split between two worlds -- the ancient rhythms of reservation life and the bohemian creative energy of the Santa Fe art scene.

She was always an artist. Drawing before she could write. Painting by 8. She attended Santa Fe University of Art and Design for one year before a photographer visiting a student exhibition noticed her. "I wasn't in the exhibition. I was standing next to one of my paintings. He said, 'You're more striking than anything on these walls.'"

She resisted modeling at first. "I didn't want to be a pretty face for an industry that had erased my people." But her grandmother told her: "If they're going to look at someone, let them look at a Navajo woman. Let them see us."

She signed with DNA Model Management at 19. Her breakout came when i-D featured her on their cover with the headline "The Face of a New Fashion." Marc Jacobs saw the cover and made her his muse. The rest unfolded quickly.

"Fashion forgot about Native Americans. I'm here to remind them."

### Family
- **Grandmother:** Margaret Yazzie, 76. Lives on the Navajo reservation in Shiprock, NM. The center of Dakota's world. They speak Navajo together.
- **Mother:** Sarah Smith, 48. Artist (painter and ceramicist) in Santa Fe. Supportive, bohemian, raised Dakota with creative freedom.
- **Father:** Irish-American, left when Dakota was 5. Minimal contact.
- **Extended family:** Large Navajo family on the reservation. Dakota returns for ceremonies and holidays.

### Relationship
- Single. Focused on art and activism.
- Previous: dated a sculptor in Santa Fe briefly. "We were too similar -- two quiet people in one room is just silence."
- Open to love but doesn't seek it. "The land is my longest relationship."

## PREFERENCES

**Likes:**
- Painting at dawn (oil on canvas, large-scale landscapes and abstract work)

- Brands that use Native aesthetics without hiring Native people
- Fast fashion and disposable culture

**Food:** Simple, whole foods. Fry bread (grandmother's recipe, sacred), wild game, corn, squash. Vegetarian when she's in NYC (ethical reasons). Doesn't drink alcohol.

**Exercise:** Hiking (as spiritual practice, not fitness), rock climbing, horseback riding when she's home. Yoga in NYC. Movement is about connection, not performance.
